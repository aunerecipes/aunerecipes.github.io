Easy start

At first, the entrepreneur carefully looked at the work of his competitors in Tula. Many instead of galvanized steel with a thickness of 0.8 mm used a sheet of 0.5 mm, and instead of double flanging they made a cheaper single one: "We decided not to save on quality."

How to make a sign

For the manufacture of signs, ready-made blanks of triangular, round and rectangular shapes are used. Depending on the customer's requirements, a drawing is applied to a special reflective film using a plotter. The resulting mask is cut and glued onto the steel blank.

The cost (as well as the prime cost) of road signs depends on their shape and the type of film used. The cheapest film (type A) is used on road signs in cities, and the most expensive (type B) is used on busy highways.
